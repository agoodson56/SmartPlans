Estimator's Review Checklist ESSENTIAL

How to Turn an AI Estimate into a Winning Bid

This section is your practical, step-by-step guide to reviewing and refining the SmartPlans AI-generated estimate before it becomes your final bid. Whether you are a junior estimator building your first proposal or a seasoned veteran looking for a systematic checklist, this guide ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

1. Understanding What SmartPlans Gives You

SmartPlans reads your drawings and specifications using a 27-brain AI engine. It counts devices, estimates cable quantities, prices materials, calculates labor, and generates a professional proposal. That is a tremendous head start, but it is not a replacement for estimator judgment.

Key Mindset: The AI estimate is a strong starting point. Your job is to verify, refine, and finalize. Think of it as a first draft written by a very fast but occasionally imperfect assistant.
What the AI Does WellWhere the AI Needs Your Help
Counts symbols quickly across dozens of sheetsMay miscount devices shown in legends or detail callouts as actual installed devices
Catches buried spec requirements humans often skipCannot judge constructability, site access, or real-world labor conditions
Applies consistent pricing across every line itemMay select the wrong manufacturer or product line if the spec is ambiguous
Generates RFIs from gaps in the drawingsCannot read the general contractor's mind about schedule, phasing, or logistics
Processes addenda and layers in scope changesMay hallucinate quantities when drawings are blurry or cluttered
Bottom Line: Trust the AI for speed and coverage. Trust yourself for accuracy and context. The combination of both is what wins bids.

2. Step-by-Step Review Process

Work through these seven checks in order. Each one builds on the previous, and skipping any of them is where estimators lose money.

A. Verify Device Counts (MOST CRITICAL)

Device counts drive everything downstream: cable quantities, labor hours, and material costs. If the counts are wrong, the entire estimate is wrong.

  1. Open your construction drawings side-by-side with the SmartPlans Bill of Materials (BOM).
  2. Pick 2 to 3 representative floors and hand-count key devices: cameras, card readers, door contacts, pull stations, speakers, WAPs, and data drops.
  3. Compare your hand counts to the AI counts for those same floors. Calculate the percentage difference.
  4. If the AI is within 5%, the counts are likely reliable across the full set. If the difference exceeds 15%, recount the entire drawing set carefully.
Common AI Counting Errors:
  • Counting devices shown in detail drawings or legends as actual installed devices
  • Missing devices on reflected ceiling plans (RCPs) that are separate from the floor plans
  • Double-counting devices shown on both architectural and low-voltage sheets
  • Confusing similar symbols across disciplines (e.g., smoke detector vs. speaker/strobe)
Tip: If the AI count is within 5% of your hand count, it is likely correct across the full drawing set. If it is off by more than 15%, do a full recount. Between 5% and 15%, spot-check additional floors before deciding.

B. Check Cable Quantities

Cable is one of the highest-cost materials on any low-voltage project. A 20% error in cable quantities can swing your bid by tens of thousands of dollars.

CheckWhat to Look ForTypical Range
Average run lengthDivide total cable footage by device count. Does the average make sense for the building size?150 to 250 ft in most commercial buildings
Conduit quantitiesAI often underestimates underground and exterior conduit. Check site plans for trenching runs.Varies widely; verify against site drawings
Backbone cableCompare backbone fiber and copper counts against the riser diagram. Count the number of risers and IDF-to-MDF connections.Should match riser diagram exactly
Waste factorEnsure a waste factor is included. Standard is 10% for cable, 15% for conduit fittings.1.10x multiplier on cable; 1.15x on fittings
Quick Cable Sanity Check: Total cable = Device count x Average run length x 1.1 (waste). If the AI total is more than 15% different from this formula, investigate the individual runs.

C. Review Material Specifications

The spec book is the law of the project. If the AI priced the wrong product line, you will either overbid (and lose the job) or underbid (and lose money).

  • Manufacturer match — Verify the AI selected the correct manufacturer. If the spec says Axis cameras but the AI priced Hanwha, correct it before generating your proposal.
  • Approved equals — Check whether the spec allows "approved equal" substitutions or is locked to a specific brand. If substitutions are allowed, you may be able to use lower-cost products.
  • Cable types — Confirm plenum vs. riser rated, Cat6 vs. Cat6A, and fiber strand counts match the spec requirements exactly.
  • OFCI items — Look for spec sections that designate equipment as Owner-Furnished, Contractor-Installed (OFCI). These items should be priced at $0 for material but still carry labor hours for installation.
Note: Use the Editable BOM feature to correct any manufacturer or pricing errors directly in the table. Changes recalculate totals in real time.

D. Validate Labor Hours

Labor is typically 40% to 60% of a low-voltage bid. The AI estimates labor using industry benchmarks, but your company's actual productivity rates may differ significantly.

Adjust for Conditions: The benchmarks above assume standard 9 to 10 ft ceilings, open access, and normal working conditions. Add 25% to 50% for high ceilings (over 15 ft), occupied spaces, secure facilities, or confined areas. Union projects may also have different productivity rates and crew composition requirements.

Most low-voltage projects require coordination with other trades. Missing a subcontractor scope item means you will pay for it out of your own pocket.

F. Review Pricing

The AI Grand Total represents the raw estimated cost based on the selected pricing tier and regional multiplier. Your final bid price will layer additional costs on top.

  • Rate Library — Apply your company's known pricing from past projects. This overrides AI estimates with your actual historical costs.
  • High-dollar items — Individually verify pricing on servers, NVRs, head-end equipment, fire alarm control panels, and generators. A $2,000 error on a single head-end unit changes your bid significantly.
  • Regional accuracy — Confirm the regional pricing multiplier is appropriate. Sacramento pricing is not the same as San Francisco pricing. Adjust the multiplier in project setup if needed.
  • Benchmark feature — If you have actuals from past projects recorded in SmartPlans, use the Benchmark feature to compare AI pricing against your real-world costs.
Important: The AI Grand Total is the raw material + labor cost. Your proposal price adds labor markup, material markup, overhead, burden, profit, and contingency on top. Make sure you understand the difference before quoting a number to a GC.

G. Finalize Exclusions & Assumptions

Exclusions and assumptions are your legal protection. A missing exclusion can cost you the entire profit margin on a project. This is not optional.

  1. Review the auto-generated exclusions that SmartPlans created from the AI analysis. Remove any that do not apply to your project.
  2. Add project-specific exclusions. Common examples: "Excludes work above 30 ft requiring scaffolding or lifts," "Assumes normal business hours access (7AM to 5PM)," "Excludes permit fees unless noted."
  3. Verify your assumptions match reality. If you assumed open ceilings but the building has hard-lid ceilings, your labor estimate is wrong. Call the GC or owner's rep to clarify unknowns.
  4. Review clarifications to ensure scope boundaries are crystal clear. Ambiguity in scope is where disputes and change orders originate.
Tip: Use the Exclusions & Assumptions Builder and load the discipline-specific templates as a starting point. Then customize for your project. It is far better to have too many exclusions than too few.

3. Common Pitfalls That Lose Money

These are the top ten mistakes that cost estimators real dollars. Every one of them has happened on actual projects. Learn from the losses of others.

#PitfallHow to Avoid It
1Not reading the spec addendaUpload all addenda to SmartPlans. The AI reads them, but you should too. Addenda frequently change quantities, products, or scope.
2Missing liquidated damages clausesRead the general conditions carefully. LD clauses can be $500 to $5,000 per day. Factor schedule risk into your contingency.
3Underestimating travel and per diemUse the SmartPlans Travel & Per Diem calculator. Account for crew size, multiple mobilizations, and realistic hotel rates.
4Forgetting permit fees and inspectionsContact the local AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction) for permit costs. Budget for re-inspection fees as well.
5Not accounting for phased constructionWorking around occupied spaces is 25% to 50% slower. Use Multi-Phase Pricing to structure phased bids properly.
6Missing prevailing wage requirementsCheck for Davis-Bacon (federal) or state prevailing wage. SmartPlans supports both; set the correct wage type in project setup.
7Underestimating conduit and pathwayConduit is the most frequently under-bid item in low-voltage work. Walk the site if possible. Add 15% to 20% contingency on pathway.
8Not including commissioning and testing laborBudget 8% to 12% of total labor hours for system testing, commissioning, and owner training.
9Forgetting warranty period costsMost contracts require 1 to 2 years of warranty. Budget for service calls, replacement parts, and return trips.
10Not reading the general conditionsWho provides temporary power? Dumpsters? Material hoisting? If the GC does not, the cost falls on you.

  • This mapping is used by all downstream analysis brains for accurate identification
Tip: If your drawings do not include a separate legend page, the AI will attempt to identify symbols from context, but accuracy improves significantly when a legend is provided.

Step 3 — Upload Floor Plans

Upload the actual drawing sheets that contain device symbols and layout information.

  • Images — JPG, PNG, WebP. Each image is treated as one drawing page.

Upload Process

  1. Click "Choose Files" or drag-and-drop files into the upload zone
  2. For PDFs, each page is automatically split into individual sheets
  3. Preview thumbnails appear showing each page that will be analyzed
  4. Remove unwanted pages (title blocks, general notes) before continuing

Step 4 — Upload Specifications

Upload specification documents so the AI can perform code compliance checking and extract product/standard requirements.

Step 5 — Addenda & Supplemental

Upload any change orders, addenda, or supplemental instructions that modify the base drawings or specifications.

  • Addenda are processed after the base drawings to layer in changes
  • The AI adjusts counts, pricing, and scope based on addendum content
  • Multiple addenda can be uploaded and are applied in order

Review all uploaded documents and confirm your project settings before launching the AI analysis.

  • Verify all uploaded files (legend, floor plans, specs, addenda)
  • Confirm project settings, disciplines, and pricing configuration
  • Remove or re-upload any incorrect files
  • Click Start Analysis to launch the 27-brain AI engine
Note: The analysis typically takes 2 to 5 minutes depending on the number of pages. A real-time progress display shows which brain is currently processing.

6b. Competitor Bid Comparison NEW in v5.0

Sometimes you want to see how your estimate stacks up against another contractor's bid. Maybe your boss got a competitor's pricing, or you want to compare your numbers against a subcontractor's quote. SmartPlans lets you upload their bid and instantly see a side-by-side comparison — showing exactly where you're higher, where you're lower, and by how much.

How to Use It (Step-by-Step)

  1. Run Your Estimate First
    You need to have a completed SmartPlans estimate before you can compare. Go through Steps 1–6 as normal. Once you see the results page with your BOM (Bill of Materials), you're ready.
  2. Get the Competitor's Bid
    You need the competitor's bid as an Excel file (.xlsx) or CSV file (.csv). This could be:
    • A bid they submitted to the same project
    • A subcontractor quote you received
    • An old estimate from a different tool
    • Any spreadsheet with item names, quantities, and prices
    The spreadsheet does NOT need to match SmartPlans' format. SmartPlans will automatically figure out which columns are which. It just needs to have some combination of: item descriptions, quantities, unit costs, or total costs.
  3. Find the Comparison Section
    Scroll down on the results page (Step 6) until you see a teal-bordered card that says "COMPETITOR BID COMPARISON". Click on it to expand it.
  4. Enter the Competitor's Name
    Type the competitor's name in the "Competitor Name" box. This is just a label so you remember whose bid you uploaded. Example: "ABC Electric" or "Johnson Controls Quote".
  5. Upload Their Bid
    Click the "UPLOAD BID" button (teal button). Select the Excel or CSV file from your computer. SmartPlans will read it automatically — you don't need to do anything else.
  6. Read the Results
    After a few seconds, you'll see the comparison broken into several parts:

    Part A — Summary Dashboard (4 boxes at the top)

    • OUR TOTAL: Your SmartPlans estimate total
    • THEIR TOTAL: The competitor's bid total
    • DIFFERENCE: How much more or less your bid is (green = you're cheaper, red = you're more expensive)
    • MATCH RATE: How many of your line items SmartPlans was able to match against theirs

    Part B — Higher/Lower Breakdown (2 cards)

    • Left card (red border): Shows how many items YOU'RE MORE EXPENSIVE on, and by how much total
    • Right card (green border): Shows how many items YOU'RE CHEAPER on, and by how much total

    Part C — Comparison Table

    This is the big table showing every matched item:

    • Item name
    • Your price vs their price
    • The dollar difference
    • The percentage difference
    • Rows are sorted with the BIGGEST differences at the top (so you see the most important variances first)
    • Green rows = you're cheaper on this item
    • Red rows = you're more expensive on this item
    • Gray rows = prices are within 5% (basically the same)

    Part D — Unmatched Items

    At the bottom, you'll see two collapsible lists:

    • "Items only in our bid" — things you included that they didn't
    • "Items only in their bid" — things they included that you didn't

    These are important! If they have items you don't, you might be missing something. If you have items they don't, you might have a more complete bid.

  7. Take Action
    Now that you can see the differences:
    • Look at the red rows (where you're higher) — can you sharpen those prices?
    • Look at items only in their bid — should you add those to yours?
    • Use the editable BOM table above to adjust your quantities and prices
    • Click "Regenerate Proposal" to create an updated proposal with your changes
  8. Clear and Try Another
    Click the "CLEAR COMPARISON" button at the bottom to remove the comparison. You can then upload a different competitor's bid to compare against.

Tips & Warnings

Tip: SmartPlans matches items by name — the more similar the item descriptions are, the better the matching works. If you see a low match rate, it might mean the competitor uses very different item names than SmartPlans. Check the "unmatched" lists to see what didn't match.
Important: Competitor bid data is NOT saved anywhere — it only exists while you're viewing the comparison. Once you refresh the page or close the browser, it's gone. This is intentional to protect competitive information. If you need to keep the comparison, take a screenshot or print the page.
What File Formats Work? SmartPlans can read Excel (.xlsx) and CSV (.csv) files. The spreadsheet needs to have columns for item names and some kind of pricing (unit cost, total cost, or both). SmartPlans automatically detects which columns are which — you don't need to rename anything.

Labor Burden

The burden rate represents the employer's additional cost on top of the base hourly wage. It covers legally required taxes and insurance that you must pay for every labor hour.

ComponentTypical %What It Covers

Formula: Loaded labor rate = Base rate × (1 + Burden%). For example: $68.90/hr journeyman × 1.35 burden = $93.02/hr loaded rate.

Markup / Margin Percentages

Markups are applied to your cost to determine the sell price for the client. This is where your profit comes from.

Markup TypeApplied ToDefaultWhat It Means
Material MarkupAll material and equipment costs50%$100 cost → $150 sell price ($50 gross profit)
Labor MarkupAll labor hours (after burden)50%$100 loaded labor → $150 sell price
Equipment MarkupRental equipment (lifts, tools, vehicles)15%$1,000 rental → $1,150 sell price
Subcontractor MarkupAll subcontractor costs (civil, electrical, fire alarm)10%$50,000 sub cost → $55,000 sell price

How the Final Bid Price is Calculated

The Financial Engine brain combines all of these settings into your grand total:

  1. Raw material cost = AI quantities × tier unit prices × regional multiplier
  2. Material sell price = Raw cost × (1 + Material Markup %)
  3. Raw labor cost = AI labor hours × hourly rate × (1 + Burden %)
  4. Labor sell price = Loaded cost × (1 + Labor Markup %)
  5. Equipment sell = Equipment cost × (1 + Equipment Markup %)
  6. Subcontractor sell = Sub cost × (1 + Sub Markup %)
  7. Direct cost subtotal = Material sell + Labor sell + Equipment sell + Sub sell + Travel
  8. G&A overhead = Subtotal × 15% (general and administrative)
  9. Profit = (Subtotal + G&A) × 10%
  10. Contingency = Total × 10% (risk buffer for unknowns)
  11. Grand Total (Bid Price) = Everything above combined
Pro Tip: Use the Bid Strategy feature on the results page to apply different markup percentages per category based on your confidence level — higher markup on uncertain items, lower on well-known costs.

20. Troubleshooting

IssueSolution
AI analysis fails or times outTry reducing the number of pages. Remove non-drawing pages (title blocks, specs).
Low confidence scoreUse the "Specific items to count" field with clear instructions. Ensure drawings are high-resolution. Upload a symbol legend.
Missing devices in countSelect the correct disciplines. The AI filters by trade to avoid cross-discipline false positives.
PDF won't uploadEnsure the PDF is under 50MB. Try splitting large PDFs into smaller batches.
Proposal formatting issuesThe proposal downloads as .docx — open in Microsoft Word for best results.
Export button not workingCheck your browser's popup blocker. Some exports open new windows.
Numbers don't match between proposal and web appFixed in v5.0. If you are running an older cached version, clear your browser cache and reload.
Supplier quote import failsEnsure the Excel file format matches the SmartPlans supplier quote template. Column headers must not be modified.
Rate library not applyingFuzzy matching requires a partial name match. Ensure saved rate names overlap with BOM item names. Check the match threshold in settings.
