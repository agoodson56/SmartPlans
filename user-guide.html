Estimator's Review Checklist ESSENTIAL

How to Turn an AI Estimate into a Winning Bid

This section is your practical, step-by-step guide to reviewing and refining the SmartPlans AI-generated estimate before it becomes your final bid. Whether you are a junior estimator building your first proposal or a seasoned veteran looking for a systematic checklist, this guide ensures nothing falls through the cracks.

1. Understanding What SmartPlans Gives You

SmartPlans reads your drawings and specifications using a 27-brain AI engine. It counts devices, estimates cable quantities, prices materials, calculates labor, and generates a professional proposal. That is a tremendous head start, but it is not a replacement for estimator judgment.

Key Mindset: The AI estimate is a strong starting point. Your job is to verify, refine, and finalize. Think of it as a first draft written by a very fast but occasionally imperfect assistant.
What the AI Does WellWhere the AI Needs Your Help
Counts symbols quickly across dozens of sheetsMay miscount devices shown in legends or detail callouts as actual installed devices
Catches buried spec requirements humans often skipCannot judge constructability, site access, or real-world labor conditions
Applies consistent pricing across every line itemMay select the wrong manufacturer or product line if the spec is ambiguous
Generates RFIs from gaps in the drawingsCannot read the general contractor's mind about schedule, phasing, or logistics
Processes addenda and layers in scope changesMay hallucinate quantities when drawings are blurry or cluttered
Identifies potential change orders from scope gapsCannot predict owner decisions or GC negotiation outcomes
Bottom Line: Trust the AI for speed and coverage. Trust yourself for accuracy and context. The combination of both is what wins bids.

2. Step-by-Step Review Process

Work through these seven checks in order. Each one builds on the previous, and skipping any of them is where estimators lose money.

A. Verify Device Counts (MOST CRITICAL)

Device counts drive everything downstream: cable quantities, labor hours, and material costs. If the counts are wrong, the entire estimate is wrong.

  1. Open your construction drawings side-by-side with the SmartPlans Bill of Materials (BOM).
  2. Pick 2 to 3 representative floors and hand-count key devices: cameras, card readers, door contacts, pull stations, speakers, WAPs, and data drops.
  3. Compare your hand counts to the AI counts for those same floors. Calculate the percentage difference.
  4. If the AI is within 5%, the counts are likely reliable across the full set. If the difference exceeds 15%, recount the entire drawing set carefully.
Common AI Counting Errors:
  • Counting devices shown in detail drawings or legends as actual installed devices
  • Missing devices on reflected ceiling plans (RCPs) that are separate from the floor plans
  • Double-counting devices shown on both architectural and low-voltage sheets
  • Confusing similar symbols across disciplines (e.g., smoke detector vs. speaker/strobe)
Tip: If the AI count is within 5% of your hand count, it is likely correct across the full drawing set. If it is off by more than 15%, do a full recount. Between 5% and 15%, spot-check additional floors before deciding.

B. Check Cable Quantities

Cable is one of the highest-cost materials on any low-voltage project. A 20% error in cable quantities can swing your bid by tens of thousands of dollars.

CheckWhat to Look ForTypical Range
Average run lengthDivide total cable footage by device count. Does the average make sense for the building size?150 to 250 ft in most commercial buildings
Conduit quantitiesAI often underestimates underground and exterior conduit. Check site plans for trenching runs.Varies widely; verify against site drawings
Backbone cableCompare backbone fiber and copper counts against the riser diagram. Count the number of risers and IDF-to-MDF connections.Should match riser diagram exactly
Waste factorEnsure a waste factor is included. Standard is 10% for cable, 15% for conduit fittings.1.10x multiplier on cable; 1.15x on fittings
Quick Cable Sanity Check: Total cable = Device count x Average run length x 1.1 (waste). If the AI total is more than 15% different from this formula, investigate the individual runs.

C. Review Material Specifications

The spec book is the law of the project. If the AI priced the wrong product line, you will either overbid (and lose the job) or underbid (and lose money).

  • Manufacturer match — Verify the AI selected the correct manufacturer. If the spec says Axis cameras but the AI priced Hanwha, correct it before generating your proposal.
  • Approved equals — Check whether the spec allows "approved equal" substitutions or is locked to a specific brand. If substitutions are allowed, you may be able to use lower-cost products.
  • Cable types — Confirm plenum vs. riser rated, Cat6 vs. Cat6A, and fiber strand counts match the spec requirements exactly.
  • OFCI items — Look for spec sections that designate equipment as Owner-Furnished, Contractor-Installed (OFCI). These items should be priced at $0 for material but still carry labor hours for installation.
Note: Use the Editable BOM feature to correct any manufacturer or pricing errors directly in the table. Changes recalculate totals in real time.

F. Review Pricing

The AI Grand Total represents the raw estimated cost based on the selected pricing tier and regional multiplier. Your final bid price will layer additional costs on top.

  • Rate Library — Apply your company's known pricing from past projects. This overrides AI estimates with your actual historical costs.
  • High-dollar items — Individually verify pricing on servers, NVRs, head-end equipment, fire alarm control panels, and generators. A $2,000 error on a single head-end unit changes your bid significantly.
  • Regional accuracy — Confirm the regional pricing multiplier is appropriate. Sacramento pricing is not the same as San Francisco pricing. Adjust the multiplier in project setup if needed.
  • Benchmark feature — If you have actuals from past projects recorded in SmartPlans, use the Benchmark feature to compare AI pricing against your real-world costs.
  • Potential change orders — Review the AI-identified change orders in Stage 8. Factor high-probability items into your contingency or bid strategy.
Important: The AI Grand Total is the raw material + labor cost. Your proposal price adds labor markup, material markup, overhead, burden, profit, and contingency on top. Make sure you understand the difference before quoting a number to a GC.

G. Finalize Exclusions & Assumptions

Exclusions and assumptions are your legal protection. A missing exclusion can cost you the entire profit margin on a project. This is not optional.

  1. Review the auto-generated exclusions that SmartPlans created from the AI analysis. Remove any that do not apply to your project.
  2. Add project-specific exclusions. Common examples: "Excludes work above 30 ft requiring scaffolding or lifts," "Assumes normal business hours access (7AM to 5PM)," "Excludes permit fees unless noted."
  3. Verify your assumptions match reality. If you assumed open ceilings but the building has hard-lid ceilings, your labor estimate is wrong. Call the GC or owner's rep to clarify unknowns.
  4. Review clarifications to ensure scope boundaries are crystal clear. Ambiguity in scope is where disputes and change orders originate.
Tip: Use the Exclusions & Assumptions Builder and load the discipline-specific templates as a starting point. Then customize for your project. It is far better to have too many exclusions than too few.

Getting Started

What Is SmartPlans?

SmartPlans is a website that reads your construction drawings for you. You upload your blueprints and spec books as PDF files. The AI looks at every page, counts all the devices, figures out how much cable you need, prices everything out, and gives you a complete bid. It does in minutes what used to take days by hand.

What Do You Need?

  • Your construction drawings saved as a PDF file.
  • Your specification book (also called "specs") saved as a PDF file.
  • Any addenda (changes to the original drawings or specs) saved as PDF files.
  • A web browser like Chrome, Edge, or Firefox.
  • An internet connection.
Tip: PDF files work the best. The AI can read both the pictures and the text inside a PDF.

Open SmartPlans

Go to https://smartplans-4g5.pages.dev/ in your web browser. You will see 8 stages across the top of the page. Each stage is a button you click when you are ready to move forward. Just go from Stage 1 to Stage 8, one at a time.

Good to know: You do not need to install anything. SmartPlans runs right in your browser. The 8 stages are: Project Setup, Symbol Legend, Floor Plans, Specifications, Addenda, Review & Analyze, Travel & Per Diem, and Results & RFIs.

Include Labor Burden Important

This is a checkbox with a percentage number (the default is 35%). Labor burden is the extra cost on top of what you pay a worker. It covers things like taxes, insurance, and health benefits. Every company pays these costs.

  1. Check the box to turn on burden.
  2. Type a percentage. The default of 35% works for most companies.
  3. SmartPlans adds this percentage to every labor dollar automatically.

Do not skip burden. If you bid without burden, you are losing about 35% on every labor dollar. On a $200,000 labor bid, that is $70,000 in costs you cannot recover. Burden is not profit — it is a real cost your company pays.

How the Bid Price Is Calculated

Here is the step-by-step math SmartPlans uses to get from the AI counts to your final bid number:

  1. Raw Material Cost
    Raw Materials = AI Quantities x Tier Prices x Regional Multiplier
    The AI counts every device. Each device gets a price based on your tier (Budget, Mid, or Premium). Then the regional multiplier adjusts for your area.
  2. Material Sell Price
    Material Sell = Raw Materials x (1 + Material Markup %)
    Your material markup (default 50%) gets added on top. This covers your costs for ordering, storing, and delivering materials, plus profit.
  3. Labor Cost (Loaded)
    Labor Cost = AI Labor Hours x Hourly Rate x (1 + Burden %)
    The AI figures out how many hours each task takes. Those hours get multiplied by the blended rate, then burden gets added on top.
  4. Labor Sell Price
    Labor Sell = Loaded Labor x (1 + Labor Markup %)
    Your labor markup (default 50%) gets added on top of the loaded labor cost.
  5. Equipment Sell Price
    Equipment Sell = Equipment Costs x (1 + Equipment Markup %)
    Rental tools like lifts and testers, with your equipment markup (default 15%).
  6. Subcontractor Sell Price
    Sub Sell = Subcontractor Costs x (1 + Sub Markup %)
    Any work done by other companies you hire, with your sub markup (default 10%).
  7. Subtotal
    Subtotal = Material Sell + Labor Sell + Equipment Sell + Sub Sell + Travel (from Stage 7)
    Everything gets added together, including travel costs from Stage 7 if you turned them on.
  8. Contingency
    Contingency = Subtotal x 10%
    A 10% safety cushion to cover surprises: extra work, wasted materials, mistakes, and things nobody saw coming.
  9. Grand Total
    GRAND TOTAL = Subtotal + Contingency
    This is your final bid number — the price that goes on your proposal.
Quick Check: After the AI builds your estimate, look at the split between materials and labor. For most low-voltage projects, materials should be 40-55% and labor should be 45-60% of the total before markup. If materials are 80% and labor is 20%, something is probably wrong.

Stage 2 — Symbol Legend

What Is a Symbol Legend?

A symbol legend is a special page in your drawings. It shows a list of little pictures (like circles, squares, and triangles) and tells you what each one means. For example, a small circle might mean "camera" and a triangle might mean "speaker." It is like a key on a map.

How to Upload It

  1. Find the legend page in your drawing set. It is usually one of the first pages.
  2. Click the upload area on this stage. A file picker will open.
  3. Pick the PDF page or pages that show the symbol legend.
  4. You will see a preview of what you uploaded. Make sure it looks right.
Why does this matter? The AI uses this legend to understand what all the little symbols on your floor plans mean. Without it, the AI has to guess — and guessing is less accurate.
Tip: This stage is optional. If your drawings do not have a separate legend page, that is okay. The AI will try to figure out the symbols on its own. But uploading a legend makes the AI much more accurate.

Stage 3 — Floor Plans

What Are Floor Plans?

Floor plans are the drawings that show the building layout and where all the devices go. They have little symbols for cameras, data drops, speakers, and everything else that needs to be installed.

How to Upload Them

  1. Click the upload area or drag your PDF file into it.
  2. Upload the full drawing set as one PDF file.
  3. SmartPlans will split each page apart automatically. You will see a small picture of each page.
  4. If any pages are not floor plans (like a title page or a table of contents), you can remove them.
Big files are okay! If your PDF is very large, SmartPlans will split it into smaller pieces automatically. You do not need to do anything special.
This stage is required. You must upload floor plans for SmartPlans to work. Without drawings, the AI has nothing to count.
Tip: Only upload pages that have actual device symbols on them. Remove title pages, general notes, and cover sheets for the best results.

Stage 4 — Specifications

What Are Specifications?

Specifications (people call them "specs") are the written instructions that come with the drawings. The specs tell you exactly what products to use and how to install them. For example, the specs might say "Use Axis brand cameras" or "All cables must be plenum-rated."

How to Upload Them

  1. Click the upload area on this stage.
  2. Pick the spec book PDF from your computer.
  3. SmartPlans will read through all the pages and find the important requirements.
Tip: This stage is optional but very helpful. The specs tell the AI exactly which products and brands to use, so your prices will be more accurate.

Stage 5 — Addenda

What Are Addenda?

Addenda are changes that were made to the original drawings or specs after they were first sent out. Think of them like corrections or updates. An addendum might say "Add 5 more cameras to the parking lot" or "Change the cable type to Cat6A." If there are addenda, they are very important because they change what you need to bid.

How to Upload Them

  1. SmartPlans will ask you: "Are there any addenda for this project?" Pick Yes, No, or Not Sure.
  2. If you pick Yes, the upload area appears. Click it and pick your addenda PDF files.
  3. You can upload more than one addendum. Each one shows up as a separate file.
  4. If you pick No or Not Sure, just move on to the next stage.
Do not skip addenda! Addenda can completely change what you are bidding. If you miss an addendum that adds 20 cameras, your bid will be way too low.
Tip: If you are not sure whether there are addenda, check with the general contractor or look at the bid documents again. It is always better to upload them than to miss them.

Stage 6 — Review & Analyze

What Is This?

This is the stage where you check everything you have entered so far, and then tell the AI to start working. Think of it like reviewing your homework before you turn it in.

What You Will See

  1. A summary of everything you uploaded: your floor plans, specs, symbol legend, and addenda.
  2. An accuracy indicator that shows green, yellow, or red:
    • Green = You uploaded everything. The AI has the best chance of being accurate.
    • Yellow = You are missing something (like specs or a symbol legend). The AI will still work, but it might not be as accurate.
    • Red = Something important is missing (like floor plans). You should go back and fix it.
  3. A text box for additional notes. Type anything else the AI should know about this project.
  4. The big "Begin Analysis" button.

How to Run the AI

  1. Review the summary. Make sure everything looks right.
  2. If the accuracy indicator is yellow or red, think about going back to upload the missing files.
  3. Type any extra notes in the notes box (optional).
  4. Click the "Begin Analysis" button.
  5. Wait while the 27-brain AI engine reads your drawings. This can take a few minutes depending on how many pages you uploaded.
  6. When the AI is done, SmartPlans automatically moves you to Stage 7.
What is the 27-brain AI engine? SmartPlans uses 27 different AI brains that each specialize in something different. One brain counts cameras. Another brain reads specs. Another brain calculates cable lengths. They all work together to build your estimate.
Be patient. The AI is reading every page of your drawings and specs. Large drawing sets (50+ pages) can take several minutes. Do not close the browser window while it is working.

Stage 7 — Travel, Per Diem & Incidentals NEW

What Is This?

This stage shows up after the AI finishes its analysis. It is where you set up travel costs if your crew needs to go to another city for the project. It also has a place for extra costs like permits, insurance, and bonding.

The reason this comes after the AI analysis (instead of at the beginning) is because the AI needs to figure out how many labor hours the project needs first. Once it knows the hours, it can help you figure out how many workers you need and how long the project will take.

What You Will See at the Top

  1. Total Labor Hours — The AI shows you the total number of labor hours it calculated. For example: "1,847 Total Labor Hours."
  2. AI Crew Breakdown — The AI suggests how many of each type of worker you need. For example: 4 journeymen, 1 apprentice, 1 foreman, 1 project manager, and how many weeks the project will take.
Good to know: The AI crew breakdown is a suggestion based on the labor hours. You can change any of the numbers when you set up your schedule below.

Enable Travel & Per Diem Costs

  1. Look for the checkbox that says "Enable Travel & Per Diem Costs."
  2. If your crew does NOT need to travel (the project is near your office), leave it unchecked and skip to the "Other Incidentals" section below.
  3. If your crew DOES need to travel, check the box. New fields will appear.

Scheduling Mode

When you turn on travel, you will see two choices for how to plan the schedule. Pick one:

Option A: "By Techs"

Use this if you already know how many workers you want to send.

  1. Click the "By Techs" radio button.
  2. Type the number of techs you plan to send. For example: 4
  3. SmartPlans automatically calculates how many work days the project will take based on the total labor hours and your number of techs.

Option B: "By Schedule"

Use this if you already know how many days you have to finish the project.

  1. Click the "By Schedule" radio button.
  2. Type the number of days you have to complete the work. For example: 15
  3. SmartPlans automatically calculates how many techs you need to finish in that many days.
Tip: If you are not sure which to use, start with "By Techs" and enter the crew size the AI suggested. Then look at the number of days it calculates. If the schedule seems too long, add more techs. If it is too short, remove some.

Number of Trips

Type how many separate trips your crew will make to the job site. Most projects need at least 2 trips:

  • Trip 1 — Rough-In: Pull cables, install backboards, mount boxes, run pathways.
  • Trip 2 — Trim-Out: Mount devices, terminate cables, test everything, and turn it on.

Big projects might need 3 to 5 trips. Each trip adds more hotel, food, and travel costs.

Travel Costs

Fill in each cost field. If a field does not apply, leave it at $0.

Other Incidentals

These are extra costs that are not travel, but they need to go somewhere in your bid. Fill in any that apply:

Live Cost Summary

At the bottom of Stage 7, you will see a live cost summary. This shows every line item and the math behind it. As you change numbers in the fields above, the summary updates instantly.

The last line shows the Stage 7 Total — this is the total of all travel and incidental costs. This number gets added to your overall bid in Stage 8.

Moving to Stage 8

  1. Review the cost summary to make sure everything looks right.
  2. Click the "View Results" button to go to Stage 8.
Tip: Travel costs are often the make-or-break part of out-of-town bids. Double-check your number of trips and crew size. Forgetting a trip (like assuming one trip when you really need three) is one of the fastest ways to lose money.

Stage 8 — Results & RFIs

This is the big results page. It has everything the AI found, plus all the tools you need to turn the estimate into a final bid. There are a lot of sections here, so let us go through each one.

AI Analysis Text

What Is This?

At the top, you will see the full AI analysis. It is a long report written by the AI that explains everything it found in your drawings and specs. It has a table of contents so you can jump to different sections.

  1. Read through the analysis, or use the table of contents to jump to the parts you care about.
  2. Look for anything that seems wrong or surprising. The AI might have misunderstood something in the drawings.

Math Validation Banner

This banner tells you if all the numbers in the estimate add up correctly. If the math checks out, you will see a green message. If something does not add up, you will see a warning so you can investigate.

Section Completeness Banner

This banner shows how complete the AI's analysis is. If the AI could not find enough information for some sections, it will tell you here. This helps you know which areas need extra attention from you.

BOM Table (Bill of Materials)

What Is This?

BOM stands for "Bill of Materials." This is a big table that lists every single item in your estimate. It shows the item name, how many you need (quantity), the cost per item, and the total cost.

How to Edit It

  1. Look through the table to see all the items.
  2. If any number looks wrong, click on it. The number turns into a box you can type in.
  3. Type the correct number.
  4. Press Enter or click somewhere else. The totals update right away.
Tip: You can click on any quantity or any price to change it. This is the fastest way to fix mistakes the AI made. The totals recalculate instantly.

Exclusions & Assumptions Builder

What Is This?

Exclusions are things that are NOT included in your bid. For example: "We are not doing any work above 30 feet." Assumptions are things you are guessing to be true. For example: "We assume the ceilings are easy to reach." Clarifications are extra notes that explain what you mean. These protect you if there is a disagreement later.

How to Use It

  1. You will see three tabs at the top: Exclusions, Assumptions, and Clarifications. Click the tab you want to work on.
  2. Click "Load Defaults" to start with a standard list that most projects need.
  3. Click "Auto-Generate" to let the AI create items based on what it found in your drawings.
  4. Read through the list. Add anything that is missing by clicking the add button. Remove anything that does not apply.
Do not skip this! Exclusions are your legal protection. If you forget to exclude something, you might have to do that work for free.

Bid Strategy Card

What Is This?

Bid Strategy lets you set different markup percentages for different categories of work. You can also set how confident you feel about each category.

How to Use It

  1. Look at each category (like "Cameras" or "Cabling").
  2. Set a markup percentage for materials and labor in each category.
  3. Set a confidence level: High, Medium, or Low.
    • High confidence = You trust the numbers. Less extra money is added.
    • Low confidence = You are not sure about the numbers. More extra money is added to protect you.
  4. Click "Apply Strategy" to update your totals.
  5. Click "Reset" if you want to go back to the default markups.

Bid Phases & Alternates

What Is This?

Sometimes a project is split into parts. The "Base Bid" is the main part that the client definitely wants. Then there are "Alternates" — extra things the client might want to add, remove, or make optional. This tool lets you organize your estimate that way.

How to Use It

  1. Your main estimate starts as the Base Bid.
  2. Click to create an Add Alternate — this is extra work that costs more money.
  3. Click to create a Deduct Alternate — this is work that gets removed to save money.
  4. Click to create an Optional Alternate — work the client might or might not want.
  5. Assign BOM categories to each phase. For example, put "parking lot cameras" in an Add Alternate.
  6. Your proposal will show a summary table of all phases and their prices.
Example: Base Bid = all indoor cameras and cabling. Add Alternate 1 = upgrade to 4K cameras. Deduct Alternate 1 = remove the parking lot cameras. Optional Alternate 1 = add a visitor management kiosk.

Potential Change Orders NEW

What Is This?

A change order is extra work that was not in the original plan. For example, if the owner decides to add 10 more cameras after the project starts, that is a change order and they have to pay extra for it.

The AI looks at your drawings and specs and finds things that might turn into change orders later. It shows them in a special card on Stage 8 called "Potential Change Orders."

Where to Find It

  1. Go to Stage 8 — Results & RFIs.
  2. Scroll down past the BOM table and Exclusions section.
  3. Look for the collapsible card called "Potential Change Orders." Click it to open it.

Inside the card, you will see a table with these columns:

  • Critical — Very serious. Could cost a lot of money. Deal with this right away.
  • High — Important. Could affect your bid significantly.
  • Medium — Worth knowing about. Might become an issue.

Important: These are things the AI thinks might become change orders. They are not guaranteed to happen. Use them as a heads-up, not as a final answer. Always use your own judgment.

Competitor Bid Comparison

What Is This?

If you have another company's bid (as an Excel or CSV file), you can upload it here. SmartPlans will put their prices next to yours so you can compare them side by side.

How to Use It

Supplier Pricing Workflow

What Is This?

This is how you get real prices from your vendors and put them into your estimate. The AI gives you estimated prices, but your suppliers know the actual prices. This process lets you swap in real prices.

How to Do It (Step by Step)

  1. In Stage 8, click "Supplier BOM" to download a blank spreadsheet. This spreadsheet has item names and quantities, but no prices.
  2. Email that spreadsheet to your suppliers. Ask them to fill in their prices and send it back.
  3. When they send back their pricing (as a PDF or Excel file), click "Import Supplier Pricing" on the Stage 8 results page.
  4. SmartPlans reads their prices and updates your estimate with the real numbers.
  5. Click "Generate Proposal" again to create a new proposal with the real pricing.
Good to know: SmartPlans matches the supplier's items to your BOM by name. The more similar the names are, the better the matching works.
Tip: Always get real vendor pricing for expensive items like servers, cameras, and control panels. The AI prices are estimates — your vendor knows the real cost.

Rate Library

What Is This?

The Rate Library is like a notebook where SmartPlans saves the prices from your past projects. Later, when you start a new project, you can use those saved prices instead of the AI's guesses. The more projects you do, the better your saved prices get.

How to Save Rates

  1. After you finish an estimate in Stage 8, look for the "Save Rates from This Estimate" button.
  2. Click it. All the current prices from this project get saved to your library.

How to Use Saved Rates on a New Project

  1. Start a new project and go through Stages 1-8 as normal.
  2. In Stage 8, look for the "Apply to Estimate" button in the Rate Library section.
  3. Click it. SmartPlans replaces the AI prices with your saved prices wherever it finds a match.
  4. You can also search your saved rates, delete old ones, or update them anytime.
Tip: Your prices get more accurate over time. After 10 to 15 projects, your Rate Library becomes your best tool for winning bids because you are using real prices instead of estimates.

Actuals & Benchmarks

What Is This?

After a project is completely done and all the bills are paid, you can go back and tell SmartPlans what you actually spent. Then, on future projects, SmartPlans can tell you things like "Based on 3 past projects, this item usually costs about $X." This helps you make better guesses over time.

How to Record Your Actual Costs

  1. Go to your Saved Estimates (click "Saved" in the header bar at the top).
  2. Find the project that is finished.
  3. Click "Record Actuals."
  4. For each category, type what you actually spent on materials and labor.
  5. SmartPlans compares your original estimate to the real numbers and shows you the difference.

How Benchmarks Help You

  1. The more projects you record, the better the benchmarks get.
  2. On future estimates, SmartPlans shows messages like "Based on 3 past projects, this item averages $X."
  3. Use these benchmarks to spot-check the AI's numbers. If the AI says a camera costs $400 but your benchmarks say $350, you might want to adjust.
Important: This only works if you go back and enter the real numbers after every project. Make it a habit!

Pricing Configuration

What Is This?

These are the settings that control how SmartPlans calculates your bid price. They live in the collapsible "Pricing & Rate Configuration" section on Stage 1. You can change them for each project.

The Main Settings

SettingWhat It Means (Simple)Default
Material Markup %How much extra you charge on top of material costs. If materials cost $100 and your markup is 50%, you charge $150.50%
Labor Markup %How much extra you charge on top of labor costs. Same idea as material markup.50%
Equipment Markup %How much extra you charge on top of rental equipment costs.15%
